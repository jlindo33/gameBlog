Is Elden Ring more of the same from this stale, bloated genre?

So Elden Ring is here and it’s getting perfect scores everywhere. Everyone seems to be playing it, and it seems like a 100% lock for Game of the Year. So why don’t I care?

Yes, you read that correctly. I don’t care. I really asked myself why a game with so much hype doesn’t interest me. Do I just not get the genius of it like I hear when I tell people I don’t like Monty Python or Napoleon Dynamite? I fancy myself to be a pretty smart guy so I don’t think that’s it. Is it because it’s too hard? Absolutely not. I love challenging games. I grew up playing Mega Man 2. So it’s not that. It’s the “Souls Type” genre as a whole and that’s why I don’t care.

Here’s the problem with the genre. It’s bad. Fundamentally, at the root, these games are flawed. The mechanics are clunky, the graphics are sub par, there is little to no score and if I have to go to reddit to get a story, well then your game doesn’t have one. I’ve tried to play them. I really have. Demon Souls looked really interesting when it launched and I hated it. Much later on I tried Dark Souls 3 with the same results. My last attempt was with Bloodborne. One of my greatest friends whose opinion I trust very much told me “It’s everything a 3D Castlevania should have been”. That’s such a great sales pitch I had to pick it up. I couldn’t stand it. I feel like all three games have these fundamental flaws mentioned above that I just can’t overcome.

So is Elden Ring different? It might be, but I won’t give the genre another chance. For one, undeniable reason and the genre’s greatest flaw. The fans of “Souls Type” games are just the worst. I’ll put them up against Super Smash Brothers, New England Patriots (I’m from the Boston area for the record) and Dave Matthews fans for the most insufferable people on Earth. If you tell one of these people you don't like Dark Souls or Nioh or any other game in this genre, you instantly get a smug look immediately followed by questions. “Is it too hard for you?” or “How can you not like it?”. After explaining my reasoning they usually take some time to process, because my reasoning is fact, not opinion until someone tells me otherwise and it twists their brains into a pretzel. However, once the processing is over then comes the their explanation, which I just debunked before hand, ultimately leading to their dumb conclusion that “I just don’t get it”.

It’s exhausting dealing with these people. If you tell a Smash Brothers fan that it’s not a fighting game and it’s actually a party game, they lose their minds followed by continuing not to shower. Next time you run into a Dave Matthews fan ask them why they need to be on psychedelics to enjoy jam bands? They can’t process it. They’ll just tell you that you “need to see Dave live to get it” followed by how many times they’ve seen them. This is the same type of person and this, more than anything else, is the “Souls Type” problem. So like most things in life, unfortunately people ruin things more than the things ruining themselves…

If you got this far, thank you for allowing me to get on my soapbox. I feel better.
