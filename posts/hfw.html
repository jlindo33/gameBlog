Horizon Forbidden West

An excellent addition that doesn't quite live up to the original.

First and foremost, following up the second best game of the last generation was not going to be an easy task. However, Guerrilla Games answered the bell and it was much more than I expected.

Picking up after the events of Horizon Zero Dawn we find our protagonist Aloy put into another impossible save the world type of situation while dealing with robot dinosaurs. It sounds ridiculous but believe me it works. Without spoiling either game, I’ll just say Aloy has to venture into the forbidden west. A place that is ruled by the bloodthirsty Tenakth who are still bitter about a long standing feud with the Sundom, the capital of the east. They believe the people of the Sundom, the Carja, are responsible for a genocide that was performed against their people during the “red raids”. I really want to talk more about the story and didn’t realize writing my opinion while being “spoiler free” was going to be this difficult, because the story is really good. I suppose you’ll just have to play it on your own to find out.

The first thing you notice is how beautiful this game is. Guerrilla Games really stepped up here. While the original is still beautiful and holds up, Forbidden West has a level of detail I’ve only seen in The Last of Us: Part II (the best game of the last generation if you were wondering). I’m playing on a Playstation Pro and was truly impressed. They improved on everything in this aspect. Aloy’s trademark hair looks even better. Her facial expressions are almost life-like. The supporting characters and even the NPC’s look just as good. The scenery is almost photo realistic, whether you’re in a tropical area or a more desolate area, nothing is ignored. I am beyond excited to play on a next gen console when I decide to buy one.

They also improved on the mechanics. Many of the best weapons return. Her signature bow is back along with some other favorite weapons and traps, including my personal favorite the trip caster. Also note that they nerfed this trap and I’m fine with that. It was way too strong in the first game and this almost makes you utilize your entire repertoire of tricks.

However no great sequel can be made without adding new mechanics right? Forbidden West gives us a few to master. First, we have a grappling hook, or hook shot for us olds. This opens up the platforming in the game, which like any open world game will eventually get tedious. However where this mechanic shines is in the combat. Being able to quickly escape or get to higher ground is an added benefit for the player. The next new mechanic we see is a glider. This is the new trend in open world games thanks to The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild. However Horizon makes it their own in a very cool way and again, this is a bonus to the player. It is a very satisfying feeling jumping off of a mountain summit and gliding towards your next objective without worrying about your stamina. The last new mechanic is we are able to swim, or more accurately swim underwater. Again the people at Guerrilla have come up with a unique way to do this and it gives us some great story arcs and level design..

All of these additions make Horizon Forbidden West an excellent game. I enjoyed it from beginning to end and I think I did all the side quests as well. Which is exceptionally rare for me. However, where the game could have improved is with its story. Let me say that the story is very well written. It has light moments, heart breakers and a very good twist. The supporting cast is excellent along with the actors who played them. But, trying to follow up the events of Zero Dawn might be an impossible task. The twist that game takes to learn who Aloy is while explaining what happened in their “post post” apocalypse was brilliant. So is Horizon Forbidden West a must play? Absolutely. Get it right now if you haven’t already, and if you’re new to the franchise, play Horizon Zero Dawn first. You’ll thank me later and it will just make your experience with this game that much better.
