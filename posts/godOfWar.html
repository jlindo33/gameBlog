God of War Ragnarok

Very excited to see where this is going

Santa Monica Studios gave God of War fans a little treat over the weekend. A cinematic trailer for God of War Ragnarok was dropped. We don’t get to see all that much, but we do see Kratos doing Kratos things and Atreus supporting him. However what really interests me is how Santa Monica is going to adapt Ragnarok into their own story.

With the predecessor, they did an excellent job adapting Norse mythology into their own lore. God of War ended implying that Atreus was indeed the trickster God Loki. Sorry for the no “spoiler alert” but the game has been out for like four years now. If you haven’t played it, that’s on you!

By no way, shape or form am I an expert in Norse mythology. Admittedly I don’t know much because like most of you the only Norse mythology you know is what you learned from the Thor movies. Which I haven’t seen because DC > Marvel. Anyway, I knew a giant wolf was involved in the story and we showed said wolf at the end of the trailer. After some quick googling I learned, which makes the last games ended even cooler, that the wolf could be Fenrir. Son of Loki and a driving force in Ragnarok. I can’t wait to see how they adapt this into their universe.

Santa Monica is setting themselves up the same way Guerrilla Games did with Horizon Forbidden West. You touch the sun once, it’s hard to get back there. But I do have faith that this game will surpass the 2018 release just like 2018’s version blew away the previous trilogy, which I enjoyed watching my roommate play at the time but found the combat incredibly boring.
