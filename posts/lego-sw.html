LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga devilers and I'm having fun!

So, I don't play many LEGO games. For the same reason I don't use TikTok, I'm almost 40. The last one I played my wife and I played together, and it was LEGO: The Harry Potter Collection, which we enjoyed. However, I'm a big Star Wars fan and a Star Wars game that has been delayed for like four years is finally here. I had to try it.

I knew what I was getting into. If you have ever played one of these games before you know they're usually a third person action/adventure with bright colors and that incredible LEGO humor. This delivers on of all that and more. Maybe I'm enjoying the humor more because I am such a big fan of the franchise, but I found myself at times laughing out loud at the scenes.

The game gives you the options to start playing through each of the trilogies. However, you can't skip around. If you want to play "The Empire Strikes Back", you need to complete "A New Hope" first. Which I like. Naturally there is only ONE way to play through this game, and if I have to explain that to you well, I don't know what else to say.

The combat is simple, which again I knew what I was getting into and not expecting much more than what I got. But it is very tight for what it is. I do like being able to switch between characters while you're not alone. Swapping over to R2-D2 so you can unlock a door is a neat mechanic. I did laugh that I could swap over to C-3P0, take my torso off my legs and get through an air duct to find a secret.

My biggest complaint so far would be with the flying mechanics. Ok, secret. I'm an inverted Y axis guy. Yes, yes I feel the disgust on your face. I don't know where it started, if I had to guess maybe Star Fox back on the SNES. Oh, and it took me forever to get over when it comes to aiming. I was always that guy that would have to adjust the settings and piss my friends off. I was able to get over aiming, but for some reason flying is still very hard for me without the inverted Y. Which this game does not give you the option. With controls that were made for children to master, I almost couldn't complete the trench run. Pretty embarrassing...

As of right now, I'm only halfway through Empire, which means I have a long way to go. I feel like I need to be in the right mood to turn this one, but when I do I throrougly enjoy it. If you are a Star Wars fan looking for some light easy fun at the expense of the franchise, you should definitely give this a try. My hope is that the humor alone will give me the motivation to get through all nine episodes, and maybe a Mandalorian DLC? I think I'm asking for too much.
